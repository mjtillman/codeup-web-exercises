1. Which National Parks are in Texas?
Texas is home to the Big Bend and Guadalupe Mountains National Parks. Additionally, most of Padre Island, which stretches from Corpus Christi to Brownsville, is designated as a National Seashore.
2. Do I have to pay any fees to enter the National Parks?
Yes, but they last for 1 week. There are also annual passes for each of the parks, or a national "America the Beautiful" pass.
3. Is there anywhere to stay overnight in the parks?
There are campsites in all three of the National Parks in Texas. If you wish to camp, there are nightly camping fees in addition to the entrance fees.
4. How do I get to the National Parks?
Both Big Bend and the Guadalupe Mountains National Parks are located in the remote region of West Texas. El Paso is the closest large city to either, being 1.5 hours from Guadalupe, or nearly five hours from Big Bend.

Padre Island National Seashore is a short distance from Corpus Christi. It is recommended that you drive your own vehicle to any of these parks, as flying, transporting camping equipment, and renting a car could be quite costly.
